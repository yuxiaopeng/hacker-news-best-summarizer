## 4. AI采纳与索洛生产力悖论

**原文标题**: AI adoption and Solow's productivity paradox

**原文链接**: [https://fortune.com/2026/02/17/ai-productivity-paradox-ceo-study-robert-solow-information-technology-age/](https://fortune.com/2026/02/17/ai-productivity-paradox-ceo-study-robert-solow-information-technology-age/)

罗伯特·索洛在1987年提出的“生产力悖论”——即信息技术（IT）进步并未立即转化为经济增长——似乎正在人工智能（AI）领域重演。尽管企业在人工智能方面投入巨资，标准普尔500指数公司也频繁提及AI，但更广泛的生产力提升尚未显现。

国家经济研究局（NBER）近期对6000名高管进行的一项研究显示，大多数人认为AI对运营的影响微乎其微，采纳AI的高管平均每周仅使用1.5小时。近90%的人表示，在过去三年中，AI对就业或生产力没有影响。阿波罗全球管理公司首席经济学家托斯滕·斯洛克指出，人工智能“无处不在，除了宏观经济数据中”，这包括“七巨头”之外的就业、生产力和利润率数据。

尽管一些研究，例如麻省理工学院2023年的研究，表明AI显著提升了员工绩效，但其他研究结果则更为保守。麻省理工学院2024年的一项研究预测，未来十年生产力将“令人失望地”仅增长0.5%。造成这一差距的原因包括员工对AI实用性的持续不信任，以及IBM等公司优先招聘人类以培养未来的领导人才。

然而，经济学家认为这种模式可能会逆转，类似于20世纪90年代信息技术繁荣最终带来的生产力激增。埃里克·布林约尔夫森和穆罕默德·埃尔-埃里安看到了AI驱动生产力飞跃的早期迹象，即GDP增长与就业增长脱钩。斯洛克设想了一种“J型曲线”效应：初期放缓，随后是指数级增长。

与过去的信息技术不同，由于激烈的竞争，AI工具现在已唾手可得。因此，未来的AI生产力不仅取决于技术本身，更关键在于“生成式AI如何在经济的不同部门中被使用和实施”。

Specialist online investigator Greg Squire, with the US Department of Homeland Security, faced immense challenges in locating "Lucy," a 12-year-old girl being abused, whose images were shared on the dark web with all identifying features removed. Despite knowing she was in North America, and a regional sofa clue narrowing the search to 40,000 people across 29 states, progress was stalled, and Facebook's facial recognition technology was unhelpful.

The critical breakthrough came from a mundane detail: an exposed brick wall in Lucy's bedroom. Squire contacted the Brick Industry Association, where expert John Harp identified the specific "Flaming Alamo" brick, sold from the late 1960s to mid-1980s. Harp’s crucial insight that "heavy bricks don't go very far" allowed the team to narrow the sofa customer list to those within a 100-mile radius of his factory in the US south-west, reducing the suspects to 40-50 people.

By cross-referencing this list with social media and using Harp to assess if specific properties (clad externally) would likely contain the distinctive brick internally, they pinpointed one address. State records confirmed Lucy lived there with her mother's boyfriend, a convicted sex offender. He was arrested immediately, having abused Lucy for six years, and received a sentence of over 70 years.

Squire later struggled with his mental health due to the nature of his work but sought help. Years later, he met Lucy, now an adult, who expressed her deep gratitude, calling her rescue "a prayer answered."
