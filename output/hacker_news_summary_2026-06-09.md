Ultimately, the article concludes that a mix of financial engineering, the severe compute shortage, and xAI's construction expertise are all shaping its evolving identity.

**原文链接**: [https://mimo.xiaomi.com/blog/mimo-tilert-1000tps](https://mimo.xiaomi.com/blog/mimo-tilert-1000tps)

Xiaomi, in collaboration with TileRT, has unveiled MiMo-V2.5-Pro-UltraSpeed, a 1-trillion-parameter (1T) AI model capable of achieving an unprecedented 1000 tokens per second (TPS) generation speed on commodity GPUs. Announced on June 8, 2026, this breakthrough significantly elevates AI reasoning capabilities, enabling real-time, instantaneous responses for complex tasks.

Access to the MiMo-V2.5-Pro-UltraSpeed API is application-based and limited to a two-week promotional trial from June 9 to June 23, 2026. Priced at three times the cost of MiMo-V2.5-Pro, it promises approximately ten times the generation speed, with approved users also receiving free chat access during the trial.

This speed is touted as a paradigm shift, transforming AI applications. It facilitates advanced reasoning paths like Best-of-N and Tree Search, dramatically boosts coding agent productivity by eliminating latency bottlenecks, and enables 1T models to participate in time-critical real-time decision loops, such as high-frequency trading, anti-fraud, and life-saving surgical assistance.

The remarkable performance is a result of "extreme model-system codesign." On the model side, MiMo employs FP4 quantization for MoE Experts to reduce memory footprint and bandwidth, coupled with DFlash, an innovative block-level masked parallel prediction for speculative decoding that maximizes accepted tokens per verification round. TileRT's contribution includes an ultra-low-latency inference system with a revolutionary execution model, featuring Persistent Engine Kernels and Warp Specialization, meticulously optimized to eliminate execution gaps and perfectly align with MiMo's algorithms. This synergy allows the 1T model to achieve 1000+ TPS on a single standard 8-GPU commodity node.

For community engagement, Xiaomi has open-sourced the MiMo-V2.5-Pro-FP4-DFlash checkpoint on HuggingFace.
