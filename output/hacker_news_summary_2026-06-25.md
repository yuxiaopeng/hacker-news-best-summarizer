## 1. We’re making Bunny DNS free

**原文标题**: We’re making Bunny DNS free

**原文链接**: [https://bunny.net/blog/were-making-bunny-dns-free/](https://bunny.net/blog/were-making-bunny-dns-free/)

Bunny.net has announced that Bunny DNS is now entirely free, eliminating all DNS query fees. This strategic shift is driven by their mission to make the internet faster and ensure fundamental DNS infrastructure is accessible to everyone, removing the stress of complex pricing tiers and unpredictable bills.

Previously a successful product handling 200 billion queries monthly for 300,000 domains, Bunny DNS originated as the advanced routing engine powering Bunny CDN. Now, users receive free DNS hosting for up to 500 domains per account, with no query limits, per-request billing, or hidden features—including smart records and health monitoring. While a standard $1/month platform minimum spend still applies, DNS usage itself incurs no additional charges.

Bunny.net now positions DNS as the core unifying product for its platform. It features new tools like automatic zone scanning for easy migration and offers seamless integration with other services through 1-Click Acceleration for CDN deployment and 1-Click Security (Bunny Shield) for DDoS protection, consolidating performance, security, and routing.

Beyond making it free, Bunny DNS has also received significant enhancements, including default IPv6 support, the implementation of DNSSEC with NSEC Black Lies for privacy-preserving validation, and expanded support for modern record types like HTTPS, SVCB, and TLSA. These changes aim to empower developers to build faster, safer, and more resilient applications without arbitrary limits.

## 5. An entire Herculaneum scroll has been read for the first time

**原文标题**: An entire Herculaneum scroll has been read for the first time

**原文链接**: [https://scrollprize.org/firstscroll](https://scrollprize.org/firstscroll)

For the first time, an entire Herculaneum scroll, PHerc. 1667 (Scroll 4), has been virtually unwrapped and read from beginning to end, without physical intervention. Sealed since the 79 AD Vesuvius eruption, this breakthrough overcomes the cruel bargain where scrolls survived but were too fragile to open without destruction.

The process involved high-resolution X-ray microtomography at ESRF, reconstructing the wound sheet inside, flattening it digitally, and using machine learning to detect faint traces of ancient ink. Papyrologists then transcribed the text.

PHerc. 1667 reveals a Stoic philosophical treatise on ethics, dating to the 2nd century BC, discussing human nature and moral progress, and mentioning Aristocreon, a disciple of Chrysippus. Fragmentary passages offer new insights after two millennia.

Beyond this scroll, the research established a robust, scalable method. For PHerc. Paris 4 (Scroll 1), higher-resolution imaging made ink directly visible in 3D, confirming earlier readings. For PHerc. 139, the title and author were identified as "Philodemus, On Gods, Book 8," an Epicurean work.

This achievement is a victory for open, global science, championed by the Vesuvius Challenge. The data, code, and methods are openly available, allowing a worldwide community, largely recruited from challenge contestants, to contribute. Hundreds more scrolls await, and the demonstrated method is ready to unlock their contents, bringing ancient thought back into the light.
