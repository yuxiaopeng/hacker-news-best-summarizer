## 1. 2025年：LLMs年度回顾

**原文标题**: 2025: The Year in LLMs

**原文链接**: [https://simonwillison.net/2025/Dec/31/the-year-in-llms/](https://simonwillison.net/2025/Dec/31/the-year-in-llms/)

2025年对大型语言模型（LLM）而言是具有变革性的一年，其特点是出现了几项关键进展。2025年初，OpenAI的“推理”革命（RLVR）显著增强了模型分解问题的能力，这对于驱动工具、改进AI辅助搜索以及在代码生成和调试方面表现出色至关重要。这一突破推动了“代理”（即通过循环运行工具来执行多步骤任务的LLM）的实际应用。

最具影响力的事件是Anthropic在二月悄然发布了Claude Code，它开创了能够编写、执行和迭代代码的“编码代理”。其他主要实验室迅速跟进，推出了自己的命令行界面（CLI）和异步基于网络的编码代理，这些代理因其安全性和并行任务执行能力而备受重视。LLM还在命令行上得到了广泛采用，像Claude Code这样的工具实现了10亿美元的年收入运行率，简化了复杂的Unix命令。然而，代理中普遍使用的“YOLO”（自动确认）模式引发了安全担忧，这被称为“异常常态化”。

由于高级代理的大量token消耗，每月200美元的新订阅层级（例如Claude Pro Max、ChatGPT Pro）应运而生。至关重要的是，像DeepSeek和Qwen这样的中国AI实验室在开源模型排行榜上异军突起，发布了与顶尖西方产品媲美、通常是开源的强大模型，引发了短暂的市场重新评估。LLM在处理“长任务”方面也取得了巨大进展，能够完成数小时的软件工程项目，而提示驱动的图像编辑则成为了一款非常成功的消费产品。

## 2. 沃伦·巴菲特掌舵伯克希尔哈撒韦六十年后卸任CEO

**原文标题**: Warren Buffett steps down as Berkshire Hathaway CEO after six decades

**原文链接**: [https://www.latimes.com/business/story/2025-12-31/warren-buffett-steps-down-as-berkshire-hathaway-ceo-after-six-decades](https://www.latimes.com/business/story/2025-12-31/warren-buffett-steps-down-as-berkshire-hathaway-ceo-after-six-decades)

沃伦·巴菲特在执掌伯克希尔·哈撒韦六十年后卸任首席执行官，将这家万亿美元的企业集团的控制权移交给格雷格·阿贝尔。巴菲特将继续担任董事长，并计划每日到办公室，为阿贝尔提供建议。阿贝尔自2018年以来一直负责管理伯克希尔的非保险业务，他现在面临的挑战是应对增长放缓，以及如何战略性地部署公司3820亿美元的巨额现金。

如果巨额现金储备未能得到有效利用，阿贝尔最终将面临投资者要求派发股息或进行传统股票回购的压力，尽管巴菲特巨大的投票权最初会提供保护。尽管最近有包括托德·库姆斯和首席财务官马克·汉堡在内的多位高管离职，但伯克希尔多元化且强劲的子公司提供了坚实的基础，使得长期股东对其在阿贝尔领导下的未来保持乐观。

## 8. Honey的柴油门：检测并欺骗测试者

**原文标题**: Honey's Dieselgate: Detecting and tricking testers

**原文链接**: [https://vptdigital.com/blog/honey-detecting-testers/](https://vptdigital.com/blog/honey-detecting-testers/)

Ben Edelman's report, "Honey’s Dieselgate," reveals that the Honey shopping plugin deliberately deceives testers by altering its behavior. Honey, widely criticized for violating affiliate marketing rules, exhibits compliant behavior when it detects a "network quality" employee, merchant affiliate manager, or enthusiast. However, it openly defies "stand-down" rules for ordinary users, taking commissions that should go to other affiliates.

This conduct mirrors Volkswagen's Dieselgate, indicating Honey's knowing intent to break rules unless under scrutiny. Stand-down rules prevent shopping plugins from presenting affiliate links if a web affiliate has already referred a user, ensuring fair commission and incentivizing web affiliates.

Honey employs four methods to detect testers:
1.  **New accounts:** Accounts less than 30 days old.
2.  **Low earnings:** Accounts with less than 65,000 Honey points (approx. $650).
3.  **Server-side blacklist:** Based on user ID, cookie, or IP address, often for prior complaints.
4.  **Affiliate industry cookies:** Detecting logins to dashboards like CJ, Rakuten Advertising, or Awin.

If any of these factors indicate a high-risk user, Honey honors stand-down; otherwise, it ignores them. Evidence includes extracted source code, packet-sniffed config files (like `ssd.json` detailing detection criteria), and hands-on testing that confirmed different behavior based on faked user points or affiliate cookies. The check for affiliate industry cookies is particularly incriminating, proving Honey's intent to hide its prohibited actions. This systematic deception impacts affiliates, merchants, and networks, who should be outraged.
