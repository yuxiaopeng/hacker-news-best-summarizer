## 11. Tennessee man jailed 37 days for Trump meme wins settlement after lawsuit

**原文标题**: Tennessee man jailed 37 days for Trump meme wins settlement after lawsuit

**原文链接**: [https://www.fire.org/news/victory-tennessee-man-jailed-37-days-trump-meme-wins-835000-settlement-after-first-amendment](https://www.fire.org/news/victory-tennessee-man-jailed-37-days-trump-meme-wins-835000-settlement-after-first-amendment)

Retired Tennessee law enforcement officer Larry Bushart has won an $835,000 settlement after being jailed for 37 days over a Facebook meme. Represented by the Foundation for Individual Rights and Expression (FIRE), Bushart filed a federal civil rights lawsuit against Sheriff Nick Weems, Investigator Jason Morrow, and Perry County, Tennessee.

Bushart's post, shared after the September 2025 assassination of Charlie Kirk, was a pre-existing meme accurately quoting Donald Trump's "We have to get over it" statement about a school shooting. The meme specifically referenced a 2024 school shooting at Perry High School in Iowa. Despite this, Sheriff Weems obtained an arrest warrant, claiming the meme could be a threat to Perry County High School in Tennessee, over 500 miles away. Weems later admitted he knew the meme referred to an incident in a different state but omitted this context from the warrant application.

Larry spent over a month in jail on a $2 million bond, losing his post-retirement job and missing personal milestones. His case gained national attention, leading to his release. FIRE senior attorney Adam Steinbaugh stated that no one should be jailed for a harmless meme simply because authorities disagree with its message. The settlement aims to underscore the importance of protecting First Amendment rights and holding law enforcement accountable for their violations. The article also highlights other free speech cases FIRE has taken on following Kirk's assassination.
