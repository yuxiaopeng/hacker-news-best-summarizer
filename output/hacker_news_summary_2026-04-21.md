**原文链接**: [https://letsdatascience.com/news/atlassian-enables-default-data-collection-to-train-ai-f71343d8](https://letsdatascience.com/news/atlassian-enables-default-data-collection-to-train-ai-f71343d8)

Atlassian has enabled default data collection from its cloud products to train its artificial intelligence (AI) models, a move that has sparked privacy concerns among users. The company will use data from products like Jira and Confluence to enhance AI-powered features, including "Atlassian Intelligence," which offers capabilities like summarization and content generation within their platforms.

While Atlassian states that sensitive data, such as private content from sandboxes and certain admin-controlled fields, will be excluded, the broad scope of default collection raises questions for organizations handling sensitive information or those operating under strict data privacy regulations like GDPR. The company emphasizes its commitment to privacy and security, noting that data will be de-identified and used in aggregate to prevent individual identification.

Users retain the option to opt out of this data collection. However, the default "opt-in" setting shifts the responsibility to individual users and administrators to actively manage their privacy preferences, rather than requiring explicit consent. This strategy aligns with a growing trend among tech companies leveraging vast user data to accelerate AI development, often prioritizing innovation over user-centric privacy defaults. The article highlights the ongoing tension between technological advancement and data privacy in the AI era.
