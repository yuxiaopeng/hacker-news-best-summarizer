**原文标题**: Artemis II crew take “spectacular” image of Earth

**原文链接**: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/ce8jzr423p9o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/ce8jzr423p9o)

正在前往月球半途的阿尔忒弥斯2号乘员组，从他们的猎户座飞船上拍摄到了“壮观的”地球高分辨率图像。在乘员组完成了跨月轨道切入点火，进入飞向月球的轨道后，任务指挥官瑞德·怀斯曼拍摄了这些照片，其中包括一张名为“你好，世界”的图像。这标志着自1972年以来，人类首次离开地球轨道。

怀斯曼最初在曝光设置上遇到困难，但像他的船员一样，后来也“黏在窗户上”不停地拍照，甚至要求提供清洁用品来擦拭窗户。

## 5. The threat is comfortable drift toward not understanding what you're doing

**原文标题**: The threat is comfortable drift toward not understanding what you're doing

**原文链接**: [https://ergosphere.blog/posts/the-machines-are-fine/](https://ergosphere.blog/posts/the-machines-are-fine/)

The author argues that the true threat of AI in academia is not a dramatic machine takeover, but a "slow, comfortable drift toward not understanding what you're doing" among human researchers.

Through the analogy of two PhD students, Alice and Bob, the author illustrates this danger. Alice engages in traditional, challenging learning, developing deep understanding. Bob, using an AI agent for every task from summarization to debugging and writing, produces equally publishable papers. However, Alice gains fundamental knowledge and critical thinking, while Bob remains a novice lacking internal comprehension, despite identical external output. Current academic metrics, focused on quantifiable results like paper count, fail to distinguish between their vastly different learning outcomes.

The article emphasizes that for fields like astrophysics, the *process* of scientific inquiry – training minds and fostering critical thought – is paramount, often more valuable than the specific results themselves. AI, by bypassing this process, undermines the core purpose of scientific training. Matthew Schwartz's experiment with an AI writing a physics paper reinforced this: the AI produced errors and fabrications, which only a deeply knowledgeable human supervisor could detect due to years of hard-won understanding. Stronger AI won't eliminate the need for this human oversight; it will only make the lack of it harder to perceive.

The author concludes that neither full adoption nor outright prohibition of AI is the answer. The real concern is creating a generation of researchers who can achieve results and navigate peer review by pressing buttons, but lack foundational understanding, critical intuition, and the ability to explain their work from first principles. This compromises the "only part of science that wasn't replaceable in the first place": its people and their cultivated intellect.

## 7. FAA 对无人机的飞行限制，旨在将拍摄 ICE（移民和海关执法局）的行为定为犯罪。

**原文标题**: The FAA’s flight restriction for drones is an attempt to criminalize filming ICE

**原文链接**: [https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2026/04/faas-temporary-flight-restriction-drones-blatant-attempt-criminalize-filming-ice](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2026/04/faas-temporary-flight-restriction-drones-blatant-attempt-criminalize-filming-ice)

文章由法学实习生拉吉·甘比尔撰写，批评了美国联邦航空管理局（FAA）发布的一项前所未有的全国性飞行限制，该限制生效日期为2026年1月至2027年10月。这项编号为FDC 6/4375的限制，禁止私人无人机操作员在距离任何国土安全部（DHS）的“移动资产”3000英尺范围内飞行，包括移民和海关执法局（ICE）和海关与边境保护局（CBP）的车辆。电子前沿基金会（EFF）和媒体组织认为，这是一种公然将拍摄执法行为定为犯罪的企图，并严重侵犯了第一修正案的权利。

与通常只持续数小时的临时飞行限制（TFR）不同，这项TFR持续21个月，覆盖全国范围。违规者将面临刑事和民事处罚，以及无人机被扣押或销毁的后果。文章强调了其在实践中的不公平性，因为ICE特工经常使用无标记车辆，且不事先发出警告，这使得无人机操作员几乎不可能避开禁区并遵守规定。

该TFR被认为是违宪的，它侵犯了第一修正案记录执法行为的权利，以及由于缺乏公平通知而侵犯了第五修正案的正当程序保障。它还违反了FAA自身的规定，因为该机构未能明确说明需要实施该限制的危险，也未能提供媒体联系方式。

EFF认为，该TFR发布的时机，正值反ICE抗议期间，并且在平民录像揭露官员不当行为的事件之后，旨在保护联邦特工免受审查。EFF要求撤销该TFR，强调了记录执法行为对问责制和透明度的重要作用。

## 8. 穴居人：何必多用，少用管用。

**原文标题**: Caveman: Why use many token when few token do trick

**原文链接**: [https://github.com/JuliusBrussee/caveman](https://github.com/JuliusBrussee/caveman)

适用于 Claude Code 和 Codex 的 "Caveman" 插件旨在通过采用简洁的“穴居人式”沟通风格，大幅减少 AI 响应的 token 使用量。它旨在平均削减 65% 的输出 token（据报道可节约 22-87%），同时保持 100% 的技术准确性。

这种效率带来多项益处：响应时间加快约三倍，通过消除冗长语言提高可读性，以及由于输出 token 减少而显著节省成本。这一概念得到了 2026 年一篇论文的支持，该论文指出简洁性可以提高大型语言模型（LLM）的准确性。

在操作上，该插件的工作原理是剔除填充词、客套话、模糊词语以及冠词（“a”、“an”、“the”）。至关重要的是，它会保留所有技术术语、代码块和准确的错误信息，以确保不丢失任何关键信息。它只影响输出 token，不触及 AI 的内部推理过程。安装过程很简单，通过简单的命令即可完成，并且可以通过诸如“/caveman”或“talk like caveman”之类的短语触发。

## 9. German implementation of eIDAS will require an Apple/Google account to function

**原文标题**: German implementation of eIDAS will require an Apple/Google account to function

**原文链接**: [https://bmi.usercontent.opencode.de/eudi-wallet/wallet-development-documentation-public/latest/architecture-concept/06-mobile-devices/02-mdvm/](https://bmi.usercontent.opencode.de/eudi-wallet/wallet-development-documentation-public/latest/architecture-concept/06-mobile-devices/02-mdvm/)

The German implementation of eIDAS, specifically its Mobile Device Vulnerability Management (MDVM) concept for high-assurance digital identities (PID), relies heavily on platform-specific security mechanisms from Apple and Google. The system's primary goal is to ensure robust authentication and protect user keys on mobile devices against sophisticated attackers, addressing the challenge of mobile devices lacking prior security certification.

To counter known mobile device vulnerabilities, the MDVM continuously monitors device and app security posture, identifies device classes (model, OS, patch level, Hardware Key Store), and tracks known vulnerabilities. If a user device is found to be insecure, its use for high-assurance eIDAS functions, such as binding credentials to keys, is prevented.

The system gathers critical signals for this assessment. For Android, it utilizes **Android KeyAttestation** to get hardware-enforced information on HKS type, device model, OS version, bootloader state, and app integrity. Additionally, it leverages **Android PlayIntegrity Verdicts**, which are Google's proprietary assessments of device trust (e.g., "MEETS_STRONG_INTEGRITY") and app authenticity, relying on a Google backend to detect compromised devices. iOS DC Device Check is mentioned for Apple devices.

This deep integration means that the operational security and functionality of Germany's eIDAS implementation on mobile devices are intrinsically linked to the verification services and platform security provided by Apple and Google, including their proprietary backend systems, thus requiring their respective account ecosystems to function.
