**原文标题**: American importers and consumers bear the cost of 2025 tariffs: analysis

**原文链接**: [https://www.kielinstitut.de/publications/americas-own-goal-who-pays-the-tariffs-19398/](https://www.kielinstitut.de/publications/americas-own-goal-who-pays-the-tariffs-19398/)

基尔政策简报分析了2025年美国关税，结论是这些关税是一个“乌龙球”，因为美国进口商和消费者几乎承担了全部成本。这项研究利用了来自逾2500万笔交易（总价值近4万亿美元）的货物层面数据，发现大约96%的关税负担转嫁给了美国买家，而外国出口商仅承担了大约4%。这导致了关税“近乎完全地”转嫁到美国进口商品价格上。

## 12. What came first: the CNAME or the A record?

**原文标题**: What came first: the CNAME or the A record?

**原文链接**: [https://blog.cloudflare.com/cname-a-record-order-dns-standards/](https://blog.cloudflare.com/cname-a-record-order-dns-standards/)

On January 8, 2026, Cloudflare's 1.1.1.1 DNS resolver experienced a widespread outage due to a subtle change in how it ordered records in DNS responses. A memory optimization inadvertently caused CNAME (Canonical Name) records to sometimes appear *after* final A/AAAA records, instead of before them, when merging partially cached CNAME chains.

This reordering broke specific DNS client implementations, such as `getaddrinfo` in glibc (common on Linux) and certain Cisco ethernet switches. These clients process records sequentially, expecting CNAMEs to appear first to correctly resolve alias chains. When A records came first, they were ignored, leading to resolution failures or device reboots. Other clients, like `systemd-resolved`, parse all records into an unordered set before processing, avoiding this issue.

The incident highlighted a 40-year-old ambiguity in RFC 1034 (1987), which states responses may be "prefaced" by CNAMEs but lacks normative language ("MUST" or "SHOULD"). While the RFC clarifies that order within Resource Record Sets (RRsets) is insignificant, it doesn't explicitly define the ordering of different RRsets (like CNAMEs vs. A records) within a message section.

Cloudflare has reverted the change, committing to ensure CNAMEs appear before other records. To prevent future confusion and enhance protocol clarity, they have submitted an Internet-Draft to the IETF, proposing explicit requirements for CNAME ordering in DNS responses.
